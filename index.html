About Me

I'm a Machine Learning Engineer with a deep interest in crafting intelligent systems that not only solve real-world problems but do so at scale and with reliability. At modlee, I focus on building and deploying recommendation systems, enhancing backend infrastructure, and experimenting with data abstraction techniques to improve model accuracy. My work often sits at the intersection of research and engineering—where ideas turn into tools, and prototypes evolve into production systems.

Before modlee, I pursued my M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, with a focus on machine learning, deep learning, and algorithms. Along the way, I interned with InterDigital’s Emerging Technologies Lab, where I designed compression architectures for wireless communications—an experience that strengthened my ability to approach problems from both theoretical and systems perspectives.

Outside the world of code and research papers, I’m usually on a bike trail or hiking route, trying to chase those famous Californian sunsets. I’m an unapologetic Manchester United fan, a longtime cricket nerd, and generally drawn to most things that involve a ball and a bit of competition. I’ve always believed that the kind of energy and obsession I bring to my hobbies bleeds into my work ethic. Yes, it's a bit of a cliché—but like all clichés, there's some truth to it.

This website is my little corner of the internet. It’s a place where I bring together my professional work, side projects, and personal interests—a window into the guy you might want to work with, collaborate with, or just talk football and neural nets with.

Feel free to reach out via the contact email or phone number listed—whether it's about work, ideas, or even the latest Premier League drama. I'm currently based in Mountain View, California, and always happy to connect locally or virtually.
